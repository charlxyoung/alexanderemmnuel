OMO IS YOU AND I, AND ALL THAT HUMANITY REPRESENTS.

ÓMÓ

At fourteen Omo had a clear picture of her dream future. Her mother tells her "life is a function of choices," so she sticks to this truism. Every decision she took leads her to another... until she had exhausted all her chances, and yet faraway from her destination.

In her journey of self-determination: from the Sediments of Sun City, through the Fiery Galadima Forest - traversing the Sand Dunes and Barchans of the Sahara and the Boorish Waves of the Mediterranean to the Piedmont Region of Modern Italy, Omo's story is that of courage, self-discovery, encapsulated in the intricate tapestry of a mother's undying love for her child.

theAuthor

With sardonic wit and unmatched creative expressions, Alexander Emmanuel Ochogwu delves into incisive subjects through his writings. As a master of satire and socio-political euphemisms, most his works border on gender, relationships, family life and security.

Alexander Emmanuel Ochogwu, an Air Force Flight Lieutenant, Strategist, Poet and Writer is the author of The Diary of a Boy Soldier and Omo; as well as collections of personal and academic essays and poems to his credit. His debut, The Diary of a Boy Soldier received both local and international encomiums. Omo made the list of Daily Trust Newspaper Most Anticipated Book of 2018, amongst other pre-published recognitions. Amongst other things, the author is a Peace, Security and Conflict Studies expert, academic, teacher, lover of arts and music. He lives in Abuja and Port Harcourt.

Meanwhile, he is his working on Adunni, another masterpiece, and sequel to the highly anticipated novel, Omo.
